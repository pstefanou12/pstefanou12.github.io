Overview

UFC Fight Night 267 heads to the Toyota Center in Houston, Texas on Saturday, February 21, 2026, with a middleweight main event that carries significant title implications. Former champion Sean Strickland returns from a year-long absence to face the surging Anthony Hernandez in a bout that could determine the next challenger for Khamzat Chimaev's middleweight crown.

The main card features several intriguing matchups including Houston native Geoff Neal's homecoming fight against Serbian knockout artist Uros Medic, rising featherweight Melquizael Costa taking on veteran Dan Ige, and heavyweight grappler Serghei Spivac facing Ante Delija. The prelims are stacked with prospects and veterans looking to make statements, highlighted by undefeated Cuban Yadier del Valle and TUF veterans clashing in the women's flyweight division.

This card showcases the UFC's depth, with multiple fighters on the cusp of breaking into rankings and several veterans fighting to stay relevant in their respective divisions.


Main Card Picks

Sean Strickland vs. Anthony Hernandez

This main event features two middleweights with everything to prove and everything to lose. Former champion Sean "Tarzan" Strickland (29-7) returns after a turbulent year that included losing his title rematch to Dricus du Plessis and serving a suspension stemming from a physical altercation at a Tuff-N-Uff event. Now 34 and cleared to compete after completing anger management, Strickland finds himself at a career crossroads. A loss would likely push him out of the top five and significantly diminish his path back to championship contention.

Standing across from him is Anthony "Fluffy" Hernandez (15-2, 1 NC), one of the UFC's most underappreciated success stories. After dropping two of his first three UFC fights, Hernandez has been nothing short of spectacular, riding an eight-fight win streak—the longest active winning streak in UFC middleweight history without receiving a title shot. His most impressive victory came when he became the first man to stop Roman Dolidze in nearly 20 fights, a feat that eluded even top-ranked Nassourdine Imavov. A victory over the former champion would almost certainly earn Hernandez a shot at Khamzat Chimaev's belt.

Stylistically, this presents a classic grappler versus striker matchup. Hernandez brings strong grappling credentials and excellent cardio but has shown limitations in his striking game. Strickland, meanwhile, is known for his relentless volume striking, championship-level cardio, and improved takedown defense displayed in his rematch with du Plessis.

Pick:

Method:

While Hernandez has been impressive during his win streak, the competition level and stylistic challenges presented by Strickland represent a significant step up. Hernandez's biggest win came over Brendan Allen last February in Seattle, a close decision where a couple of grappling exchanges determined the outcome. However, Allen was the cleaner striker throughout that fight and was winning the ground-and-pound exchanges. The critical mistake Allen made was fishing for a submission instead of maintaining positional dominance and damage—a mistake Strickland is unlikely to repeat.

In Hernandez's fight against Michel Pereira, he didn't find his groove until later rounds when Pereira began to tire. This pattern is troubling when facing Strickland, who possesses limitless cardio and has demonstrated improved takedown defense. In his rematch with du Plessis, Strickland successfully got back to his feet when taken down, showing he's addressed previous defensive grappling vulnerabilities.

The fundamental issue for Hernandez is that he's never faced a striker of Strickland's caliber. Strickland's high-volume, technically sound striking combined with his championship cardio creates a nightmare matchup for a grappler who struggles to get into rhythm against elite strikers. Over the course of five rounds, Hernandez will have opportunities to secure takedowns, but maintaining control and threatening submissions against a motivated, well-prepared Strickland will prove extremely difficult. Expect Strickland to control the striking exchanges, stuff or escape takedowns, and cruise to a decision victory that keeps him in the title picture.

Geoff Neal vs. Uros Medic

This welterweight co-main event features a Houston homecoming for Geoff "Handz of Steel" Neal (16-7) against Serbian knockout artist Uros "The Doctor" Medic (12-3). Once undefeated in the Octagon and riding a five-fight winning streak with notable victories over Belal Muhammad, Niko Price, and Mike Perry, Neal's career took a devastating turn starting in late 2020. Since then, he's gone just 3-5, battling inconsistency inside the cage while privately fighting drug addiction and alcoholism—struggles that began during the COVID-19 pandemic shortly after a life-threatening health crisis. Now sober and refocused, the 35-year-old views this hometown fight as the beginning of a genuine comeback.

Standing in his way is the surging Uros Medic, who had the most active year of his career in 2025, competing three times. The 32-year-old Serbian has never seen a decision in his 15 professional fights, and he enters this bout on a two-fight knockout streak. Most alarmingly for Neal, Medic ranks #1 in the UFC for average knockdowns per 15 minutes among active welterweights—a terrifying statistic for any opponent, let alone one stepping up to face a ranked fighter for the first time.

This bout represents diverging career trajectories. Neal desperately needs a signature win to prove his sobriety and renewed focus have translated to octagon success. Medic needs to validate his power against a ranked veteran and announce himself as a legitimate contender in the welterweight division.

Pick:

Method:

While Neal's comeback story is compelling, the reality of his recent performances tells a concerning tale. His lone victory in his last four fights came over Rafael dos Anjos, and that win was tainted by an RDA injury in the first round. On paper, Neal won, but realistically, he's riding a three-fight losing streak into this matchup. He's struggled with activity, missed weight, and most recently suffered a brutal knockout loss to Carlos Prates.

The age and momentum factors heavily favor Medic. At 32, he's three years younger than the 35-year-old Neal and enters with considerable wind in his sails following two first-round knockout victories. This is a fighter on a legitimate run toward title contention, facing an opponent who appears to be on the decline.

Neal has absorbed significant damage in his recent fights, and the wear and tear is evident. Combined with his personal struggles over the past several years, there are legitimate questions about whether he still has the hunger and durability required at this level. Medic's knockout power and activity advantage make him a live threat every second the fight is standing.

This fight won't go the distance—neither fighter's style allows for it. But given Neal's recent damage accumulation, inconsistency, and the questions surrounding his current commitment level, Medic should be able to land the decisive blow. Expect the Serbian to continue his knockout streak and make a statement in his step-up fight against a ranked opponent.

Dan Ige vs. Melquizael Costa

This featherweight bout pits veteran Dan "50K" Ige (19-10) against surging Brazilian Melquizael "The Dalmatian" Costa (25-7) in a classic gatekeeper versus rising contender matchup. The 34-year-old Ige represents a significant step up in competition for Costa while simultaneously being vulnerable. The Hawaiian veteran of 29 professional fights finished 2025 at 1-1, suffering a split decision loss to former Bellator champion Patricio Pitbull in July before rebounding with a knockout victory over Sean Woodson in April. Now in the define-your-legacy phase of his career, a loss to an unranked Costa would severely damage his standing in the division.

Costa, meanwhile, is riding the wave of his career. After closing out 2024 on a high note, "The Dalmatian" went 4-0 in 2025, bringing his current win streak to five fights. He kicked off the year with a first-round submission against Andre Fili before taking unanimous decisions over Christian Rodriguez and Julian Erosa. In December, the Brazilian scored the most impressive highlight of his UFC career when he knocked out Morgan Charriere with a spectacular head kick. A victory over a ranked veteran like Ige would be the breakthrough result Costa needs to crack the featherweight top 15 legitimately.

The stakes are clear: Ige must prove he still belongs among the division's elite, while Costa needs to validate that his recent success translates against established competition.

Pick:

Method:

Costa is entering this fight with undeniable momentum, riding a five-fight win streak and truly coming into his own in the UFC. In contrast, Ige has been a mixed bag of results lately and hasn't produced a breakthrough performance at the top of the division. The consistency factor alone favors the Brazilian.

When examining Ige's recent body of work, the concerns become more apparent. His knockout victory over Sean Woodson back in April 2025 was a controversial stoppage, and there's a legitimate argument that he could be 0-4 in his last four fights. Ige doesn't possess a specific dominant skillset that controls octagon action—he's well-rounded but not elite in any particular area.

The physical matchup also favors Costa. Although the two fighters' reach is nearly identical, Ige is shorter than Costa, and that size differential should be apparent throughout the fight. Costa's recent improvements in combining his striking with submission threats make him a more complete and dangerous fighter than the version of himself who first entered the UFC.

While Ige's experience could help him survive some dangerous moments and extend the fight into later rounds, Costa's momentum, size advantage, and superior current form should allow him to control the majority of the action. Expect Costa to pick apart Ige over 15 minutes and earn a decision victory that propels him into the featherweight rankings.

Serghei Spivac vs. Ante Delija

This heavyweight clash features two fighters at critical junctures in their UFC careers. Serghei "Polar Bear" Spivac (17-6) sits at #9 in the rankings but finds himself in a precarious position. After suffering a devastating TKO loss to Jailton Almeida in January 2025, the Moldovan rebounded with a decision win over Waldo Cortes-Acosta in June. However, remaining in the top 10 requires consistency, and a loss here would likely cement his status as a permanent gatekeeper. Conversely, a decisive finish could thrust him back into conversations with elite names like Ciryl Gane and Tom Aspinall. Spivac's elite grappling makes him a legitimate top-10 heavyweight, but he remains boom-or-bust against the division's best.

Ante "Walking Trouble" Delija (26-7) enters with everything to prove after a rocky start to his UFC tenure. The 2022 PFL Heavyweight Champion made a spectacular debut last September by demolishing former title challenger Marcin Tybura, only to stumble in a controversial and chaotic knockout loss to Cortes-Acosta in November. Now 1-1 in the UFC, the Croatian star—a protégé of legendary Mirko Cro Cop—needs to demonstrate that his PFL dominance translates to the UFC's upper echelon. He's garnered an 86% community pick on Tapology, signaling strong public confidence, though sportsbooks remain closer to even on the matchup.

This fight represents diverging paths: Spivac fighting to maintain his ranking and relevance, while Delija seeks to prove his debut performance was no fluke and that he belongs among the heavyweight elite.

Pick:

Method:

Spivac's skillset presents significant vulnerabilities that Delija is perfectly positioned to exploit. Spivac has shown a troubling pattern of being knocked out quickly by power punchers—Tom Aspinall and Ciryl Gane both dispatched him with relative ease. Even more concerning, he struggles on the ground against elite grapplers like Jailton Almeida and has been controlled by fighters like Marcin Tybura. This inconsistency highlights the fundamental issue: Spivac is an example of how thin the current heavyweight division truly is.

Delija, meanwhile, is better than his 1-1 UFC record suggests. His knockout loss to Waldo Cortes-Acosta in November 2025 was controversial and chaotic, not a reflection of a fundamental skill deficit. His demolition of Tybura—a fighter who gave Spivac problems—demonstrates that Delija possesses the striking power and technique to trouble top-10 heavyweights.

As a Mirko Cro Cop protégé, Delija brings Croatian striking pedigree and knockout power that should overwhelm Spivac's defensive limitations. Spivac's grappling prowess won't be enough to neutralize Delija's advantages, as the Croatian has shown solid takedown defense in his career. Expect Delija to land the cleaner, harder shots early and secure a knockout victory that validates his place in the UFC's heavyweight division and questions whether Spivac truly belongs in the top 10.

Jacobe Smith vs. Josiah Harrell

This welterweight bout features an increasingly rare and compelling matchup: undefeated versus undefeated. Jacobe "Cobe" Smith (11-0) has been nothing short of spectacular since entering the UFC, finishing every single opponent including a knockout of Preston Parsons and a submission of veteran Niko Price. The 30-year-old two-time NJCAA wrestling champion and Oklahoma State All-American represents one of the welterweight division's most dangerous rising prospects. Currently ranked #33 in the UFC welterweight rankings, Smith is ascending rapidly, and a dominant victory over another unbeaten fighter would cement his status as a legitimate dark horse contender in 2026.

Standing across from him is Josiah "Muscle Hamster" Harrell (11-0), a 27-year-old making his UFC debut carrying serious regional momentum. Despite being unranked, Harrell's perfect record and the short-notice nature of this bout add an element of unpredictability to the matchup. For Harrell, this represents a massive opportunity to announce himself on the sport's biggest stage, though the circumstances—debuting against an established UFC finisher—could hardly be more challenging.

This fight carries significant implications for both men. For Smith, it's an opportunity to add another finish to his highlight reel and continue his march toward ranked competition. For Harrell, it's the chance to shock the welterweight division and prove he belongs in the UFC conversation immediately.

Pick:

Method:

The circumstances surrounding this fight heavily favor Smith. Harrell is taking this bout on short notice against a top welterweight prospect—a scenario that rarely ends well for the debuting fighter. The combination of limited preparation time and the challenge of facing an opponent with established UFC success creates an enormous disadvantage that's difficult to overcome, regardless of talent.

Smith has systematically finished every opponent he's faced in the UFC, demonstrating not just skill but the ability to impose his will and seal the deal under the bright lights. His wrestling pedigree gives him multiple paths to victory, and his finishing instincts ensure he capitalizes when opportunities present themselves. Harrell, despite his perfect record, has never faced competition at this level, and making a successful UFC debut under these conditions would require something extraordinary.

The matchmaking itself tells the story—the UFC is clearly positioning Smith for continued ascension and using this fight to showcase his talents. Harrell is being fed to a surging prospect, and while upsets certainly happen in MMA, everything about this setup suggests a decisive Smith victory. Expect Smith to utilize his wrestling to control position, overwhelm Harrell with his experience and physicality, and add another knockout to his perfect record. This should be a showcase performance that keeps Smith's name trending upward in the welterweight rankings.

Zach Reese vs. Michel Pereira

This middleweight matchup features two fighters at very different points in their careers. Zach "Savage" Reese (10-2) is a physically imposing specimen at 6'4" with a 77" reach, making him one of the most size-advantaged middleweights in the division. Currently ranked #43, Reese is coming off a strong recent run and faces his most high-profile assignment yet. A victory over the entertaining Pereira—a former welterweight mainstay—would firmly place him on the divisional radar heading into 2026.

Michel "Demolidor" Pereira (31-14) is one of the UFC's great entertainers, a highlight-reel striker known for his acrobatic and unorthodox techniques. However, the Brazilian veteran has struggled recently, most notably being systematically broken down by Anthony Hernandez in his last outing. At 31-14 and having moved between weight classes throughout his career, this represents a crucial stay-relevant fight. A loss to Reese would push him further down the pecking order and raise questions about his future in the UFC.

After suffering a controversial knockout loss to Azamat Bekoev in January 2025, Reese finished the year strong with two consecutive victories, including a second-round rear-naked choke submission of Jackson McVey—a fight Reese took on short notice, demonstrating his readiness and conditioning. Pereira, conversely, enters on a three-fight losing streak and at a crossroads in his career.

Pick:

Method:

The momentum and career trajectory heavily favor Reese in this matchup. While Pereira remains a dangerous striker capable of producing highlight-reel moments, his recent three-fight losing streak reveals fundamental issues that Reese is perfectly positioned to exploit. It's become increasingly clear that Pereira is more focused on being a performer than seriously pursuing championship aspirations, which shows in his approach and preparation.

Cardio represents Pereira's Achilles heel. He consistently struggles to maintain high output past the first round, and opponents who can weather his early flurries have systematically broken him down in later rounds. His loss to Kyle Daukaus was particularly telling about where he currently stands in his career—unable to impose his will on mid-tier competition over the course of a full fight.

Reese demonstrated impressive conditioning and commitment when he took the McVey fight on short notice and still secured a second-round submission. This shows a fighter who stays in shape and ready to compete, contrasting sharply with Pereira's questionable commitment level. The size advantage Reese possesses at middleweight, combined with Pereira's known cardio issues, creates a recipe for a grind-it-out decision victory.

Pereira appears to have one foot out the door, and Reese is the type of hungry, physically imposing fighter who will capitalize on any waning motivation. Expect Reese to survive Pereira's flashy early offense, then systematically wear him down over 15 minutes to earn a decision victory that continues his upward trajectory in the middleweight division.

Prelims Picks

Chidi Njokuani vs. Carlos Leal

This welterweight prelim bout features two strikers at different career stages. Chidi "Bang Bang" Njokuani (25-11) remains a dangerous proposition at 37 years old. The veteran possesses one of MMA's most devastating elbow attacks and an 80" reach that makes him a legitimate threat to end any fight without warning. However, he's clearly on the wrong side of the age curve and has become known as a streaky fighter who alternates between winning and losing runs.

Carlos "The Lion" Leal (22-7) is the younger man at 31 and enters as the slight betting favorite, reflecting his more recent activity and upward trajectory throughout 2025. Currently ranked #44 to Njokuani's #40, the Brazilian has faced significantly tougher competition in recent years with matchups against Shara Magomedov, Makhachev Mukhammed, and Sabidou Sy. A finish over the dangerous Njokuani would serve as a resume-building victory that nudges him into the welterweight rankings conversation.

This fight represents a classic gatekeeper versus rising contender dynamic, with Njokuani trying to prove he's still relevant while Leal looks to establish himself among the division's upper tier.

Pick:

Method:

Njokuani has established a clear pattern as a gatekeeper who experiences streaks—winning 2-3 fights before losing 2-3 fights in cycles. While his striking remains dangerous, his recent performances raise concerns about his ability to compete with rising contenders. Most notably, his split decision victory over Rhys McKee stands out as problematic. McKee is widely considered not UFC-caliber, and Njokuani's inability to beat him more decisively suggests declining effectiveness against even lower-tier competition.

The competition level disparity between these fighters is significant. Leal has consistently faced much higher-level opposition in recent years, testing himself against the likes of Shara Magomedov, Makhachev Mukhammed, and Sabidou Sy—fighters who represent legitimate threats in the welterweight division. This experience against elite competition should have sharpened Leal's skills and prepared him for moments when the fight becomes challenging.

The age factor also plays a crucial role. At 32, Leal is closer to his athletic prime compared to the 37-year-old Njokuani, who is fighting father time as much as his opponent. While Njokuani's reach and striking power make him dangerous in exchanges, Leal's youth, more recent high-level competition, and upward trajectory should allow him to control the pace and outwork Njokuani over 15 minutes. Expect Leal to fight smartly, avoid Njokuani's power shots, and grind out a decision victory that continues his ascent in the welterweight division.

Ode Osbourne vs. Alibi Idiris

This flyweight matchup pits experience against rising talent. Ode "The Jamaican Sensation" Osbourne (13-9) is one of the division's most watchable fighters, a Jamaican kickboxer with legitimate finishing ability who brings excitement every time he enters the octagon. At 34 and currently ranked #26, Osbourne appears to be in the midst of a final push at flyweight. Despite being the fan-voted underdog, he possesses the knockout power to end anyone's night at any moment.

Across from him stands Alibi Idiris (10-1), a Kazakh prospect who is knocking on the door of the rankings. The 10-1 fighter is installed as the betting favorite and currently sits at #47 in the UFC flyweight rankings. A victory over a veteran like Osbourne would serve as validation of his push up the divisional ladder in 2026 and potentially earn him his first spot in the official top 25.

This represents a classic prospect-versus-veteran dynamic, with Osbourne fighting to remain relevant in the division while Idiris looks to use him as a stepping stone toward bigger opportunities.

Pick:

Method:

While Osbourne brings excitement and knockout power, his path to victory is extremely narrow. Throughout his UFC tenure, Osbourne has demonstrated a troubling pattern: his only realistic chance against competent flyweight competition is to score a knockout in the first round. His cardio limitations become increasingly apparent as fights progress, and when he can't secure an early finish, he typically fades and loses decisions.

This creates a clear game plan for Idiris: survive the early storm and then systematically break down Osbourne over the remaining rounds. As a prospect with a 10-1 record, Idiris should possess the defensive awareness and technical soundness to weather Osbourne's early power shots while conserving energy for later rounds.

The age and career trajectory factors also favor Idiris. Osbourne, at 34, is fighting the clock and showing signs of decline, while Idiris represents the future of the flyweight division. Once Osbourne's early explosiveness wanes—typically around the midpoint of round one or early in round two—Idiris should be able to take control of the fight with superior cardio and more consistent output.

Expect Idiris to fight intelligently, avoid Osbourne's power in the opening exchanges, and then dominate the final two rounds to earn a clear decision victory that validates his status as a legitimate flyweight prospect.

Alden Coria vs. Luis Gurule

This flyweight bout features two young fighters with contrasting UFC trajectories. Alden "Cobra" Coria (11-3) is just 27 years old, making him one of the youngest competitors on the entire card. Currently ranked #33 in the UFC flyweight division, Coria enters as a significant -270 favorite, indicating the UFC views him as a genuine talent with serious upside. A dominant performance here could push him onto the rankings radar and establish him as a prospect to watch in 2026.

Luis "Grim" Gurule (10-2) faces an uphill battle as a significant underdog at +210. Despite his respectable 10-2 record showing promise on paper, his UFC tenure has been rocky. After winning his Contender Series bout via split decision—not the spectacular finish the UFC typically favors—Gurule proceeded to lose his first two outings in the promotion, raising questions about whether he truly belongs at this level. Currently ranked #44, a surprise upset would immediately elevate his profile and potentially earn him a spot in the rankings.

This matchup represents a classic prospect showcase, with Coria expected to demonstrate why the UFC has invested in his development while Gurule fights to prove he deserves to remain on the roster.

Pick:

Method:

History shows that many Contender Series fighters struggle to establish themselves in the UFC, even those who win their contracts via knockout. Gurule's path has been particularly concerning—he won his Contender Series bout by split decision rather than a dominant finish, then immediately lost his first two UFC outings. This trajectory strongly suggests he's not UFC-caliber and was perhaps given a contract prematurely.

Coria, in stark contrast, made an immediate impact with an impressive knockout in his UFC debut. That single performance is more eye-catching and significant than anything Gurule has accomplished in his entire UFC run. It demonstrated not just power but the ability to perform under the bright lights of the UFC when it matters most—exactly the quality that separates prospects from also-rans.

The massive betting line disparity (-270 for Coria) reflects the UFC's confidence in their prospect and the oddsmakers' assessment that Gurule represents a clear step down in competition. Coria is riding momentum, has shown finishing ability, and faces an opponent who has struggled against UFC-level competition from day one.

Expect Coria to showcase exactly why the UFC is high on him. His power, combined with Gurule's demonstrated vulnerabilities against UFC competition, should result in a decisive knockout victory that keeps Coria's name trending upward and raises serious questions about Gurule's future with the promotion.

Nora Cornolle vs. Joselyne Edwards

This women's bantamweight rematch carries significant divisional implications. Nora Cornolle (9-3) is 36 years old and coming off a loss to Karol Rosa. While she was once generating considerable hype in MMA, that momentum has cooled considerably—she's just 1-2 in her last three fights. Currently ranked #16 in the UFC women's bantamweight division, Cornolle notably defeated Joselyne Edwards via unanimous decision in her UFC debut back in 2023.

Joselyne "La Pantera" Edwards (16-6) represents the inverse trajectory. The 30-year-old Panamanian veteran is ranked eighth and has established herself as one of the most well-rounded fighters in the women's 135-pound division. She's riding a three-fight win streak, most recently scoring a first-round knockout of Priscila Cachoeira in August 2025. While she lost to Cornolle in 2023, Edwards has been on a tear since then, and a dominant showing in this rematch could push her back into top-five conversations.

This rematch tells the story of diverging career arcs: Cornolle trying to recapture past form while Edwards aims to prove she's evolved beyond the fighter who lost their first meeting and establish herself among the bantamweight elite.

Pick:

Method:

The key factor in this rematch is improvement and momentum. Edwards has demonstrably evolved as a fighter since their 2023 encounter. Her three-fight win streak, culminating in a first-round knockout, shows a fighter hitting her stride and developing finishing instincts that weren't as pronounced in their first meeting. Meanwhile, Cornolle's 1-2 record in her last three fights suggests stagnation or even regression.

The age differential cannot be ignored in this matchup. At 30, Edwards is firmly in her athletic prime—the sweet spot where experience meets physical capability. Cornolle, at 36, is fighting on the wrong side of the age curve, and her recent performances suggest her best days are behind her. In combat sports, six years can represent a massive gap in recovery time, durability, and overall physicality.

Edwards also carries the intangible advantage of having something to prove. Losing their first encounter undoubtedly motivated her to improve and evolve, and her subsequent win streak demonstrates she's done exactly that. Cornolle, conversely, may feel she already figured Edwards out in 2023, potentially leading to complacency or failure to adequately prepare for an improved version of her opponent.

Expect Edwards to showcase the improvements she's made over the past few years. Her more well-rounded game, combined with superior momentum and being in her prime, should allow her to control the majority of the fight. Look for Edwards to earn a decision victory that avenges her 2023 loss and positions her for a top-five opponent in her next outing.

Ramiz Brahimaj vs. Punahele Soriano

This welterweight bout features two fighters entering with identical momentum—both riding three-fight winning streaks. The near-even betting line (-110 for both fighters) reflects genuine stylistic uncertainty and the difficulty in picking a clear favorite. Ramiz Brahimaj (13-5) is a grappling-forward fighter whose submission game has given ranked opponents trouble throughout his career. Currently ranked #41 in the UFC welterweight division, a statement victory here could earn him a ranked opponent in his next outing and validate his recent success.

Punahele "Puna" Soriano (12-4) has punched his way back into relevance with a stunning first-round knockout of Uros Medic in January 2025. The Hawaiian possesses raw knockout power that makes him dangerous in any exchange. Currently ranked #25, Soriano sits significantly higher than Brahimaj in the divisional hierarchy, and this matchup against another grappler sets up beautifully for him to showcase his striking and keep his streak rolling at 170 pounds.

The contrasting styles create an intriguing dynamic: Brahimaj will look to drag the fight to the mat and hunt for submissions, while Soriano aims to keep it standing and land the fight-ending blow.

Pick:

Method:

Soriano brings a significantly more well-rounded skillset to this matchup. While primarily known for his knockout power, he's demonstrated the ability to win via multiple methods—whether by spectacular knockout or grinding out grappling-heavy decisions. This versatility gives him multiple paths to victory, whereas Brahimaj is largely dependent on successfully implementing his grappling game plan.

The competition level differential is substantial. Soriano has faced much tougher opposition since joining the UFC, testing himself against legitimate middleweights and welterweights like Brendan Allen, Roman Kopylov, and Uros Medic—the latter being a particularly impressive knockout victory. Brahimaj's current three-fight streak, while notable, comes against significantly lower-tier competition including Austin Vanderford and Billy Ray Goff. These victories don't carry the same weight or indicate readiness for a fighter of Soriano's caliber.

The grappling matchup also favors Soriano. While Brahimaj is certainly a grappling-heavy fighter with submission skills, Soriano has shown solid takedown defense and grappling acumen throughout his career. The likelihood of Brahimaj securing a submission against a bigger, stronger, more experienced fighter like Soriano seems remote.

Expect Soriano to defend Brahimaj's takedown attempts while landing the cleaner, more significant strikes on the feet. Even if Brahimaj manages to secure some takedowns, Soriano's defensive grappling and scrambling ability should allow him to limit damage and get back to his feet where he holds clear advantages. Look for Soriano to control the majority of the action and earn a decision victory that extends his winning streak and solidifies his position in the welterweight rankings.

Jean-Paul Lebosnoyani vs. Phil Rowe

This welterweight bout pits rising prospect against fading veteran. Jean-Paul "JPL" Lebosnoyani (9-2) is just 27 years old, making him the youngest fighter in this matchup, and arrives as the heavy -220 favorite despite being unranked. This betting line represents a clear signal that the UFC has significant confidence in his development trajectory. His most recent victory—a vicious first-round knockout over Jack Congdon—demonstrated the finishing ability that has the promotion excited about his future. A convincing performance here would enter him into the rankings conversation.

Phil "The Fresh Prince" Rowe (11-6) faces a critical juncture in his UFC career. The physically gifted 6'3" welterweight possesses a massive 80.5" reach, giving him physical advantages over most opponents. However, at 35 years old and currently ranked #47, Rowe has been inconsistent in the UFC. He's just 1-3 in his last four fights, most recently suffering a one-sided grappling decision loss to Seok Hyeon Ko. This fight represents a scenario where a loss could spell contract trouble, while an upset victory over the favored prospect would be one of the best results of his career.

The matchmaking tells a story: the UFC is clearly positioning Lebosnoyani for continued development and eager to showcase him against a UFC veteran, even if that veteran's best days appear to be behind him.

Pick:

Method:

The harsh reality is that Rowe's best days are behind him. At 35, fighting in one of the most physically demanding divisions, the wear and tear of a long career is evident in his recent performances. His 1-3 record in his last four UFC fights suggests a fighter on the decline, and frankly, it's somewhat surprising he remains signed to the promotion given this trajectory and the competitive nature of the welterweight division.

Lebosnoyani clearly represents someone the UFC is interested in promoting. The organization is eager to build his resume with another victory over a UFC veteran, continuing his development against progressively tougher competition. His first-round knockout of Jack Congdon showcased the finishing ability that has the promotion excited, but this fight presents a different challenge—a wily veteran with significant physical advantages.

While Lebosnoyani possesses the power and aggression to potentially score another spectacular knockout, Rowe's experience and reach should help him survive the early onslaught. A 6'3" fighter with an 80.5" reach doesn't get knocked out easily, even when his skills have diminished. Rowe's veteran instincts and physical tools will allow him to make this competitive for stretches, using his length to keep distance and survive dangerous moments.

Method:
