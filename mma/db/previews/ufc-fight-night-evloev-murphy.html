Overview

UFC Fight Night: Evloev vs Murphy arrives at The O2 Arena in London this Saturday with a lackluster card full of British and European fighters looking to get highlight wins over the bums of the UFC. The main event is a legitimate title eliminator — two undefeated featherweights colliding to determine who gets next in line for Alexander Volkanovski's belt. Besides the main event, there is very little to look forward to on this card, as has been the norm for the UFC to start off the Paramount era.

The headliner, Movsar Evloev vs. Lerone Murphy, is the best fight on the card by some distance. Evloev is the #1 featherweight in the world with a perfect 19-0 record and wins over Aljamain Sterling, Arnold Allen, and Diego Lopes. Murphy is the crowd's man — a London native who announced himself to the wider MMA world with a spinning back elbow KO of Aaron Pico at UFC 319. The collision of Evloev's grappling-heavy, pressure-based style against Murphy's dangerous creative striking is genuinely compelling, and the winner is widely expected to challenge for the title.

Below the main event, the card features a pair of unbeaten UK prospects making statement performances on home turf. Luke Riley brings an undefeated record and a track record of clean finishes to his O2 debut. Kurtis Campbell, "The Pink Panther" from Liverpool, steps onto the biggest stage of his career in his official UFC debut. Meanwhile, Michael Page continues his welterweight renaissance against British prospect Sam Patterson, and Christian Leroy Duncan — the spinning strike specialist with back-to-back Performance of the Night bonuses — looks to build toward a ranking with a win over veteran Roman Dolidze. Hopefully some crazy shit happens on this card, because there has been absolutely no promotion for this event, and most of the fights are a bunch of Contender Series debutants, whom no one gives a rat's ass about.


Main Card Picks

Movsar Evloev vs. Lerone Murphy

Movsar Evloev (19-0) enters as the #1 ranked featherweight in the UFC and one of the most quietly accomplished fighters in the division. The Russian grappler has gone 9-0 inside the Octagon, with unanimous decision wins over the likes of Aljamain Sterling, Arnold Allen, and Diego Lopes — names that represent the cream of the featherweight and bantamweight crops. His style is methodical and suffocating: controlled pressure, persistent wrestling, and the ability to dictate where the fight lives for all 25 minutes. The knock against him has always been activity — he has fought just five times in four years, and his history of pulling out of matchups has turned a chunk of the fanbase against him. The fact that he is here this weekend, though, means he is healthy and ready.

Lerone Murphy (17-0-1) is fighting in front of a hostile-but-energized crowd in his rival city and carries the kind of momentum that makes highlight-reel fans very excited. The Mancunian featherweight KO'd Aaron Pico with a spinning back elbow at UFC 319 and earned Knockout of the Year buzz in the process. He had already beaten Josh Emmett on the scorecards earlier in 2025. Murphy is a creative, dangerous striker with an arsenal that can close the show at any moment. Both men are undefeated, and this is a clear title eliminator — the winner fights Volkanovski.

Pick: Movsar Evloev

Method: Decision

It comes down to the grappling. Murphy has yet to demonstrate elite-level grappling defense in the UFC, and that is the massive question mark heading into Saturday. People got swept up in the Pico spinning elbow and started talking title shot — but let's pump the brakes. Aaron Pico was making his UFC debut. Murphy has not fought anywhere near the caliber of competition that Evloev has, and whether his wrestling holds up against a world-class grappler remains completely untested. Evloev, meanwhile, has been battle-tested against the division's best and pulled out of fights his entire career — the fact that he showed up this week means he is locked in and ready to go. This will be a twenty-five minute slog where Evloev grinds on Murphy for the full five rounds and walks away with another unanimous decision.

Luke Riley vs. Michael Aswell Jr.

Luke Riley (12-0) is one of the most exciting prospects to come out of the UK scene in recent memory. The Cage Warriors graduate signed with the UFC in August 2025 and made an emphatic statement in his debut — stopping Bogdan Grad in the second round at UFC London. He has finished the majority of his opponents, and he is doing it on the biggest stage yet this weekend in front of his home crowd at The O2. Riley is a sniper with finishing instincts, and tonight is his showcase.

Michael Aswell Jr. (11-3) is a high-output striker from Houston who is durable and willing to stand in the pocket and trade. He bounced back from a loss to Bolaji Oki in May 2025 with a first-round KO of Lucas Almeida in October. The context that matters most here: both men share a common opponent in Bogdan Grad. Aswell lost to Grad on a split decision. Riley knocked Grad out in two rounds. That shared data point puts this fight in very stark perspective.

Pick: Luke Riley

Method: KO/TKO

Riley is a sniper. He has proved throughout his time on the regional scene and in his UFC debut that he has the tools to turn someone's lights out on any given night. Aswell carries a loss to Bolaji Oki — a fighter who, frankly, does not have the most impressive chin in the world. If Oki couldn't stop Aswell, that might sound like a point in his favor — but it speaks more to the level of competition he has been navigating. Riley is a significant step up, and he is fighting in front of his home crowd with everything to prove. Aswell will not have the power or the skills to keep Riley off him, and this one gets finished inside 15 minutes.

Michael Page vs. Sam Patterson

Michael "Venom" Page (24-3) is one of the most stylistically unique fighters in the welterweight division, but his career has taken a strange turn. After a stint at middleweight where he handed Shara Magomedov his first professional loss and beat former title challenger Jared Cannonier, MVP has moved back to 170 — and the matchmaking has not done him any favors. Multiple outlets have run pieces titled "The UFC Is Wasting Michael Page," and it is hard to argue with the sentiment. The 38-year-old is fighting for relevance in a stacked division, and he knows it. His unorthodox karate-based movement and creative striking still give opponents fits, but he has been doing the bare minimum lately — outpointing fighters without ever threatening the finish.

Sam Patterson (14-2-1) is a 29-year-old British welterweight who has won four straight fights, all by first-round finish. Since moving up from lightweight, Patterson has shown genuine knockout power — he can end it with strikes and submissions — and he will have the home crowd roaring behind him at The O2. This is his first fight against an established name in the division, and a win over MVP would shoot him straight into the rankings conversation.

Pick: Michael Page

Method: Decision

The jury is out on what is happening with MVP's career. He has done the interview rounds lately talking about how the UFC is not giving him the matchups he wants, and the reality is that they tried to use him as a roadblock for Ian Garry's hype train — and Page basically let Garry have his way with him for 25 minutes. The UFC has since moved on to other contenders. Patterson is a solid prospect and a genuine can crusher who has been finishing everyone put in front of him, but he has never fought anyone on MVP's level, and that experience gap will show. Page will not get caught here. He will go in for a 1-2, bounce out, do a couple of dance moves, and cruise his way to another standard MVP performance on the scorecards.

Iwo Baraniewski vs. Austen Lane

Iwo Baraniewski (7-0) is one of the hottest prospects in the UFC light heavyweight division. The 26-year-old Polish fighter transitioned from an elite judo background and has been finishing fights with alarming regularity — three consecutive first-round KO/TKOs in the UFC, with his debut stoppage of Ibo Aslan at UFC 323 coming in just 89 seconds. He dropped from heavyweight to light heavyweight and his power has followed him down. This is only his second assignment at 205, and the UFC appears to be carefully building him up.

Austen Lane (13-7) is a former NFL defensive end who reinvented himself as an MMA fighter, now dropping from heavyweight to light heavyweight in search of better results. The move has not come at an ideal time — he is on a two-fight losing skid, having been stopped by Vitor Petrino and Mario Pinto in his last two outings. Stepping in against a fast-rising prospect who has not been out of the first round in three fights is a brutal assignment for a fighter hoping a new weight class will turn things around.

Pick: Iwo Baraniewski

Method: KO/TKO

Austen Lane is absolutely terrible and has no chin. Baraniewski is all action, all the time — he goes out there and punches people into the shadow realm, and Lane is not the man to stop that streak. Baraniewski will starch him inside the first 90 seconds of round one. This is a designed continuation fight to build the Polish prospect's UFC profile, and it will almost certainly be Lane's last appearance in the organization.

Roman Dolidze vs. Christian Leroy Duncan

Roman Dolidze (15-4) is coming off the most lopsided loss of his career, dominated by Anthony Hernandez in a fourth-round submission at UFC Vegas 109. That result snapped a three-fight winning streak that included wins over Marvin Vettori and Kevin Holland — a streak that looked more impressive on paper than it actually was. He started it by beating the dead corpse of Anthony Smith, smothered Kevin Holland with lay-and-pray for a round and got a TKO injury stoppage, then beat up the dead corpse of Marvin Vettori. The 37-year-old Georgian is now in rebound territory with his ranking slipping, and a loss to Duncan — a fighter he is likely expected to beat on paper — would put his UFC future in serious question.

Christian Leroy Duncan (13-2) is the crowd favorite and the hottest fighter on this card not named Evloev. The former Cage Warriors champion joined the UFC undefeated and has been climbing rapidly, winning three straight with increasingly spectacular finishes. He earned back-to-back Performance of the Night bonuses — a spinning back elbow finish of Eryk Anders and a spinning backfist KO of Marco Tulio. He is a highlight-reel machine fighting in front of his home London crowd, and he is looking to break into the UFC rankings with a statement win tonight.

Pick: Christian Leroy Duncan

Method: KO/TKO

Dolidze has never been that good, and the evidence is right there in his resume. He is a bear-like pressure fighter with smothering grappling skills and very little boxing prowess. His recent win streak was built on beating declining veterans and injury finishes — not exactly a ringing endorsement heading into a fight with a man who is actively getting better. Duncan has far superior striking and will be hunting for the finish from the opening bell. Dolidze will grapple aggressively, but his cardio is questionable at the best of times, and Duncan has the athleticism and creativity to weather the storm. Eventually, the Georgian runs into one of those spinning techniques and gets knocked into next week in front of the London crowd.

Kurtis Campbell vs. Danny Silva

Kurtis Campbell (8-0), known as "The Pink Panther," is an unbeaten featherweight from Liverpool who earned his UFC contract on Dana White's Contender Series in September 2025 with a first-round stoppage of Demba Seck. He has finished his last four opponents via strikes and submissions and is making his official UFC debut tonight on home turf. The pressure of a debut in front of a home crowd is real, but Campbell has looked composed and clinical in everything he has done so far.

Danny Silva (10-2), known as "El Puma," suffered his first UFC loss to Kevin Vallejos in August 2025 and is looking to bounce back. His record tells a story of a decision fighter — he has relied heavily on going the distance, with only one finish in his last five fights. This is a tough assignment: an unbeaten prospect making his debut in front of his home crowd is about the worst possible rebound fight for a fighter trying to rediscover form.

Pick: Danny Silva

Method: Decision

Campbell is a legitimate hot prospect, but this is his UFC debut and he is no longer fighting regional-level cans or Contender Series one-fight wonders. Silva has already shown he can survive with a legitimate knockout artist — he went the full distance with Kevin Vallejos, who is a vicious finisher in his own right. If Vallejos couldn't get the stoppage against Silva, it is hard to believe Campbell will manage it either. Silva will show Campbell levels tonight, drag this thing into deep water, and grind out a decision win on the scorecards.

Prelims Picks

Mason Jones vs. Axel Sola

Mason Jones (17-2) is the former Cage Warriors double champion on his second UFC stint, currently riding a six-fight winning streak. His most recent Performance of the Night bonus came via elbows-from-mount to stop Bolaji Oki in September 2025, adding another highlight to a run that also includes wins over Jeremy Stephens and Michael Pagani. Jones is a relentless pressure fighter who drags opponents into deep water with his wrestling, and he is fighting in familiar territory on a UK card.

Axel Sola (11-0-1) is a former ARES champion described as one of the most technically complete striking prospects in European MMA. The French southpaw earned his UFC contract by finishing Rhys McKee in the third round in September 2025, and he has gone 11 fights without a loss. Neither man has faced elite UFC competition yet, which makes this a genuine measuring stick for both. Jones's relentless forward pressure and wrestling against Sola's technical southpaw precision is a stylistically interesting collision.

Pick: Mason Jones

Method: Decision

Neither of these fighters have faced serious UFC competition yet, but Mason Jones has the experience edge that matters here. He is on his second stint in the promotion, has looked dominant since returning, and his first two fights back were one-sided victories over Jeremy Stephens and Bolaji Oki. Jones can both strike and grapple, and he has been here before. Sola is a promising technical prospect, but this is a step up in competition and Jones's pressure-based style is exactly the kind of fight that exposes whether a striker can hang in deep water. Jones grinds this out over the distance.

Nathaniel Wood vs. Losene Keita

Nathaniel Wood (22-6) has been on a remarkable run at featherweight, winning five straight all by unanimous decision, yet the UFC has kept him off ranked opponents at every turn. A February 2026 Bloody Elbow piece quoted him reacting to "being denied a ranked opponent yet again" and asking "What have I done wrong?" It is a fair question. Wood is a crafty veteran who grinds opponents down, but the inability to finish at this level remains the elephant in the room for his title aspirations.

Losene Keita (16-1), known as "Black Panther," has one of the more compelling backstories in European MMA — from street trouble and a brief spell in prison to becoming a multi-belt European champion before earning a UFC call-up. He enters on a five-fight winning streak with his only loss coming early in his career, and he brings a finishing edge and real power. This fight likely determines who gets their long-overdue shot at a ranked opponent next — the loser's path gets considerably harder.

Pick: Nathaniel Wood

Method: Decision

Keita is a can crusher who has been finishing people on the regional scene and is now making his UFC debut against a crafty veteran who has been here before. He will come out swinging and look for the knockout early, but Wood is going to evade the onslaught and drag this into deep water. Wood has no serious finishing ability, but he is a grinder who knows how to accumulate rounds and pull out decisions. Keita will find out that regional dominance does not automatically translate, and Wood banks another unanimous decision to keep his frustrating streak alive.

Mario Pinto vs. Felipe Franco

Mario Pinto (11-0) is an undefeated UK/Portuguese heavyweight who has been building his UFC profile fight by fight. He has run through both of his opponents in the promotion with vicious stoppages — knocking out Austen Lane in under a minute and dismantling Jhonata Diniz in the second round. He cut a hilarious promo after the Diniz win and is becoming a personality to watch in the heavyweight division. This fight was originally scheduled against Mick Parkin before Franco stepped in as a late replacement.

Felipe Franco (10-1) is a Brazilian heavyweight making his UFC debut on very short notice after Parkin withdrew. He has been active — his most recent win was a rear naked choke just five weeks ago on February 7 — but stepping into a UFC fight against an unbeaten home fighter with minimal preparation is an enormous ask. His sole professional loss came via rear naked choke in the third round, which raises cardio questions for a man who took this fight on short notice.

Pick: Mario Pinto

Method: KO/TKO

Pinto can be a serious problem in the UFC heavyweight division. Since his debut, he has completely run through his first two opponents with vicious, decisive finishes over Austen Lane and Jhonata Diniz. Franco lost on the Contender Series last fall and is now making his debut on short notice against an unbeaten fighter in front of a home crowd. He is going to get smashed. Pinto is the real deal at heavyweight, and this will be another highlight-reel finish to add to the collection.

Antonio Trócoli vs. Mantas Kondratavičius

Antonio Trócoli (12-6) is 0-3 in the UFC and has been finished in all three outings. Two of those losses came via standing guillotine choke — a specific and repeatable pattern that suggests a recurring vulnerability that opponents can gameplan around. He lost to Shara Magomedov, Tresean Gore, and Mansur Abdul-Malik, with a notable gap in his fight history between 2021 and 2024 raising further questions. At this point, he is essentially fighting to keep his UFC contract.

Mantas Kondratavičius (8-1) is a Lithuanian middleweight who signed with the UFC off Dana White's Contender Series in September 2025 after three consecutive finishes — two of them coming in under 70 seconds. He is explosive, powerful, and making his UFC debut with a clear finishing instinct. His sole professional loss was a submission in 2023, and he has since strung together four straight wins. This is a matchup designed to showcase the Lithuanian prospect.

Pick: Mantas Kondratavičius

Method: KO/TKO

Trócoli is a bum who has no business still being in the UFC. He is 0-3 in the promotion and has been finished every single time. Kondratavičius is a hot prospect making his debut after a Contender Series appearance in the fall, and he will go out there and blast Trócoli. The guillotine pattern alone should have every smart fighter salivating at this matchup. Kondratavičius will not need to look for it — he will just press forward and put the lights out early.

Louie Sutherland vs. Brando Peričić

Louie Sutherland (10-4), "The Vanilla Gorilla," is a New Zealand heavyweight who built a four-fight win streak before being submitted via heel hook in the first round by Valter Walker in October 2025. The leg lock loss exposed a potential submission vulnerability that opponents will note, and he is now looking to bounce back on a card outside his home region. His run had been built on stoppages and decisions, but the Walker result was a sharp reminder that there are holes in his game.

Brando Peričić (5-1), "The Balkan Bear," is an Australian-based heavyweight of Croatian descent who made his UFC debut with a first-round win in Perth in September 2025. Three of his four recent wins have come in the first round via ground and pound, and his pressure-based, heavy-handed style has been convincing. He has shown he can be stopped — a rear naked choke loss in 2024 — but has since rattled off three straight wins.

Pick: Brando Peričić

Method: KO/TKO

There is not a ton of data to go on with either of these heavyweights inside the UFC, but the picture is clear enough. Louie Sutherland should not be in the UFC. His record and the level of competition he has faced does not hold up under scrutiny, and the heel hook loss to Walker put a real dent in whatever credibility he had built. Peričić has one UFC fight and one win, but he looked convincing doing it. He will press forward, land something heavy, and finish this one.

Shem Rock vs. A. Al-Selwady

Shem Rock (12-2-1) is a Liverpudlian lightweight who signed with the UFC from OKTAGON in June 2025 and has been vocal about the "Scouse takeover" at UFC London. His submission game is his calling card — four of his last five wins before joining the UFC came by rear naked choke. He lost his UFC debut to Nurullo Aliev by decision, and tonight is his chance to get a win in front of the Liverpool and London fanbase that will be loud for him all night.

A. Al-Selwady (15-4), "The Pride of Palestine," earned his UFC contract by beating George Hardwick on the Contender Series in 2023. His UFC run since has been turbulent — stopped by Loik Radzhabov and then having two consecutive bouts cancelled against Bolaji Oki and Matheus Camilo throughout 2025. He has not completed a fight in almost two years. That level of ring rust heading into a fight against a motivated home fighter is a massive red flag.

Pick: Shem Rock

Method: Decision

Al-Selwady's inactivity is the story here. Nearly two years without a completed fight is almost never a good thing, and walking into a hostile crowd against a fighter who is fresher, more motivated, and fighting in front of his own people is about the worst possible scenario for shaking off ring rust. Rock is more motivated, sharper, and will have the crowd driving him forward for all three rounds. Both men are heavy grapplers, but Rock's urgency and home advantage will be the difference. Rock does not lose this fight in front of his home crowd.

Shanelle Dyer vs. Ravena Oliveira

Shanelle Dyer (6-1), "The Nightmare," is a British strawweight with an extensive Muay Thai background — reportedly over 100 bouts — who stepped up in competition and lost to Carol Foro in September 2025. This is her rebound fight, and she has the striking pedigree to keep things standing and make it uncomfortable for anyone who wants to trade.

Ravena Oliveira (7-3-1) is on a two-fight losing skid, most recently being submitted by Stephanie Luciano in October 2025. She carries power — six KO/TKO wins on her record — but has been on the wrong end of finishes in her most recent fights. Both women are coming off losses and need a win, making this a pure pick 'em rebound fight between two strawweights searching for form.

Pick: Shanelle Dyer

Method: Decision

This is pure pick 'ems. Dyer is a scrapper with a Muay Thai base who is willing to go out there and fight, and that mentality matters in a fight like this. Oliveira is not going to be tough enough to take on Dyer standing, and getting the fight to the ground consistently against someone with over 100 Muay Thai fights is easier said than done. Dyer grinds this out and gets the decision.

Melissa Mullins vs. Luana Carolina

Melissa Mullins (7-2), "No Mess," is an Oxford-based bantamweight who has had a turbulent 2025, with multiple fights crammed into a short window — her Tapology data shows three bouts on the same date in September, suggesting a tournament or multi-fight event format. Her most recent clean win came over Courtney Alex Kaye, and she brings solid ground and pound and boxing to the table. She will have the home crowd behind her tonight.

Luana Carolina (11-5), "Dread," is a Brazilian veteran who earned her UFC contract on the Contender Series in 2018 and has the broader résumé of the two. She put together a three-fight winning streak before losing decisively to Michelle Montague in Perth in September 2025 — and logged only that one fight in all of 2025. The inactivity and that loss represent real momentum questions heading into London.

Pick: Melissa Mullins

Method: Decision

Mullins has good ground and pound and some solid boxing, and she is fighting in front of her home crowd against an aging UFC vet who is running out of road. Carolina is the more experienced fighter on paper, but experience only takes you so far when your recent form is pointing in the wrong direction. Mullins has the tools to grind this out and pick up the decision win.
