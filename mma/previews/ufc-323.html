Overview

This Saturday, December 6th 2025, the UFC returns to Las Vegas for UFC 323 at T-Mobile Arena. The final PPV of the year in the United States/Canada is headlined by a bantamweight championship clash between Merab Dvalishvilli and Petr Yan, with Alexandre Pantoja defending his flyweight title against Joshua Van in the co-main event.


The card features several compelling matchups that could reshape multiple divisions, from rising prospects to legends fighting for their UFC futures.

Main Event: Dvalishvilli vs Yan II

Merab Dvalishvilli has been on a historic tear, seeking his fourth title defense this calendar year—a UFC record. Riding a generational 14-fight win streak with five victories over former champions, the Georgian champion has many fans already labeling him the sport's GOAT. His resume makes a compelling case.


On the other side, fan-favorite Petr Yan has clawed his way back into title contention after a difficult 3-fight losing skid between 2022-2023. Two of those losses came via controversial split decisions against Aljamain Sterling and Sean O'Malley. With his back against the wall at UFC 299, "No Mercy" delivered a dominant performance against Song Yadong, sparking a three-fight win streak that includes victories over Deiveson Figueiredo and Marcus McGhee.


The elephant in the octagon is their first meeting, where Dvalishvilli dominated Yan 50-45. Yan knows another loss likely ends his championship aspirations, adding palpable stakes to this rematch.


Given their styles and first fight, Yan will likely need to weather early pressure and find a spectacular knockout late. But with Merab's relentless pace and wrestling, one sloppy takedown attempt could be all Yan needs to capitalize and shock the world.

Co-Main: Pantoja vs Van

Flyweight champion Alexandre Pantoja has been unstoppable at 125 lbs, currently on an 8-fight win streak and essentially clearing out the division. Finding fresh challengers has become a UFC matchmaking puzzle.


Enter Joshua Van, the first UFC fighter born in the 21st century to earn a title shot. Since his 2023 debut, Van has fought nine times, winning eight—including a dramatic #1 contender bout against Brandon Royval at UFC 316 where a last-minute knockdown secured his shot.


While Van is a talented striker with undeniable activity, Pantoja represents a massive step up in grappling pedigree. Van hasn't faced a chain-wrestler and submission threat like "The Cannibal." This might be too much, too soon for the young contender.

Other Notable Fights

The main card features several intriguing matchups: Brandon Moreno vs Tatsuro Taira in what's essentially a flyweight #1 contender bout, Henry Cejudo vs Payton Talbott, and Jan Blachowicz vs Bogdan Guskov.


The prelims offer compelling action with Fares Ziam vs Nazim Sadhykov, Marvin Vettori vs Bruno Ferreira, and the must-watch Edson Barboza vs Jalin Turner.

Who's Next at Flyweight?

The flyweight division faces a logjam. Pantoja has already beaten top contenders—including Brandon Moreno three times and Brandon Royval twice. The Moreno-Taira winner makes the most sense for a title shot, but another Moreno matchup is hard to justify.


Manel Kape and Brandon Royval headline UFC Fight Night on December 12, but Pantoja holds wins over both. If Pantoja wins this weekend, the UFC may need to look outside the usual contenders, perhaps to Kyoji Horiguchi (currently #11 on Tapology). While controversial, options are limited. The entire division is hoping for a Pantoja loss to reopen the title picture.

Is Being Gay the Best Base for MMA?

Payton Talbott has built a reputation as a prospect-crusher but struggled against elite competition, notably losing to Raoni Barcelos at UFC 311. His subsequent decision win over Felipe Lima didn't fully silence doubters about his UFC caliber.


Now he faces former two-division champion Henry Cejudo—a clear attempt to build Talbott's name on a legend's resume. While Talbott has a real chance given Cejudo's recent form, questions remain about whether his performances justify this matchup, especially when Barcelos fights lower-ranked opponents.


Talbott's extracurricular visibility certainly plays a role in his marketability. So, I guess we will find out whether sleeping with Frank Ocean is a solid base for MMA.

Return of the Tarantula

Jalin Turner returns after briefly retiring earlier this year, citing mental health struggles from the fight game. Once a touted prospect at lightweight/welterweight, Turner's physical gifts are undeniable—length, speed, and athleticism.


But going 1-4 in his last five fights, this is likely his final UFC opportunity. A loss probably means release.


Opponent Edson Barboza, a UFC legend since 2010, is also at a career crossroads. At 39 and on a two-fight skid, this is a "win or get cut" scenario for both veterans. Expect a high-paced, action-filled striking battle that should deliver fireworks.

Summary

UFC 323 offers compelling narratives across the card. History is on the line as Merab seeks a record-breaking title defense, while Petr Yan fights for redemption and possibly his last title shot. Pantoja looks to further cement his flyweight dominance against a hungry young challenger.


From prospects to legends fighting for their careers, this card promises to deliver drama, action, and potential division-altering outcomes.
