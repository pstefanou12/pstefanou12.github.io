Overview

UFC Vegas 115 does not arrive with much hype, and looking at the card from top to bottom, that is mostly justified. The headline act has a compelling training-partner angle, the strawweight co-main is a legitimate title eliminator, and the light heavyweight showcase could be the most violent two minutes of the weekend — but past that top layer, the booking gets questionable fast. Several main card slots are occupied by fights that would barely pass muster as prelim filler.

The main event pits Renato Moicano against Chris Duncan in a rare case of training partners headlining a UFC card. Both men are BJJ black belts competing out of South Florida, which makes the stakes personal as much as professional. Moicano is ranked #10 at lightweight but has lost three straight, while Duncan enters having finished all four of his wins on his current streak. The grappling matchup is interesting on its own merits — two legitimate submission threats in a division where the top is increasingly log-jammed. Below that, Virna Jandiroba and Tabatha Ricci square off in a strawweight bout with direct title implications, and Abdul Rakhman Yakhyaev gets another opportunity to terrorize a UFC-level opponent after combining for just 63 seconds of octagon time across his first two appearances.

The rest of the card is harder to get excited about. Tommy McMillen's UFC debut is undermined by the opponent — Manolo Zecchini hasn't fought in two and a half years and has no business being on a main card in 2026. The Rafael Estevam vs. Ethyn Ewing bantamweight fight is a booking misfire, with Estevam having missed weight again and the hype around Ewing's debut win over Wellmaker arguably more inflated than his performance warranted. The prelims have a handful of roster-bubble matchups mixed in with some genuinely intriguing newcomers like Guilherme Pat and Alice Pereira. This is a card you tune in for the main event, the strawweight fight, and Yakhyaev — and check out mentally once those are done.


Main Card Picks

Renato Moicano vs. Chris Duncan

Renato Moicano (20-7-1) enters this main event ranked #10 at lightweight, but the ranking feels increasingly charitable. He has lost three fights in a row — a first-round D'arce choke to Islam Makhachev at UFC 311, a unanimous decision to Beneil Dariush in June 2025, and most recently another decision loss to Yusuf Raisov in November. The Makhachev loss is forgivable given the caliber of opponent, but the Dariush fight was alarming — Moicano looked slow, out of shape, and a step behind throughout. A decorated BJJ black belt who has shown resilience in big spots, he now needs a statement win to keep himself in title conversations.

Chris Duncan (15-2) is riding a four-fight finishing streak and has not lost in over two years. His most recent outing was a submission of Terrance McKinney via anaconda choke in under two and a half minutes — a meaningful scalp, given McKinney's kill-or-be-killed aggression. All four wins on the streak have come by submission, making Duncan a genuine grappling threat. The unusual wrinkle: both men train together in South Florida, turning this main event into an unusually personal matchup between teammates.

Pick: Chris Duncan

Method: Decision

It hurts to fade Moicano here, but the case for Duncan is hard to argue against. He is four years younger, has not lost in over two years, and is operating at the peak of his powers. Moicano, by contrast, is coming off the worst stretch of his career. The Dariush decision loss stands out — Moicano looked slow and out of shape in that fight, which raises real questions about whether he is declining or simply aging out of his physical peak. Duncan's ability to finish McKinney — a fighter who punishes anyone who is tentative or flat — shows he can handle serious aggression and still impose his game. With time on his side and a hot streak at his back, Duncan gets the better of his training partner and earns the biggest win of his career.

Virna Jandiroba vs. Tabatha Ricci

Virna Jandiroba (22-4) is ranked #3 at strawweight and arrives here off the biggest loss of her career — a unanimous decision defeat to Mackenzie Dern in October 2025 for the vacant UFC Strawweight title. She had strung together three consecutive wins before that fight, including victories over Xiaonan Yan and Amanda Lemos, and a win here would put her right back in the title conversation. The question is whether a fighter who was outworked and out-grappled in her title shot has the adjustments necessary to stay relevant, or whether she is on the downslope.

Tabatha Ricci (12-3), ranked #8 at strawweight, is coming off the biggest win of her UFC career — a second-round TKO of Amanda Ribas in July 2025. She has described this fight as the biggest of her career and views a win over the #3-ranked Jandiroba as a direct ticket to a Dern title shot. Ricci and Jandiroba share a common opponent in Xiaonan Yan, with both having lost to her, which provides a useful calibration point for where each fighter sits.

Pick: Tabatha Ricci

Method: Decision

In women's MMA, there's a troubling pattern of fighters who lose in their title shot and never quite recover — and Jandiroba's performance against Dern did nothing to suggest she'll be the exception. That title fight was a strategic disaster. Jandiroba spent stretches on the bottom, letting Dern work from guard and land from below, and never made the obvious adjustment of simply standing up. If you are pinning a jiu-jitsu specialist, you do not lie in their guard and absorb punishment — and yet that is what happened. Ricci is not a world-beater, but she is hungry, younger, and fresh off a legitimate finish of a respected opponent. Both women are at a similar skill level, but the hunger of a fighter chasing a title shot against one who may be beginning to contemplate the end of her run is a meaningful edge. Ricci gets the decision.

Abdul Rakhman Yakhyaev vs. Brendson Ribeiro

Abdul Rakhman Yakhyaev (8-0) has been nothing short of terrifying in his first two UFC appearances. Born in Chechnya and competing out of Turkey, he dispatched Raffael Cerqueira in 33 seconds and Alik Lorenz in 30 seconds — combining for just 63 seconds of total octagon time. He enters as a -3000 betting favorite, which tells you everything you need to know about how the oddsmakers see this playing out.

Brendson Ribeiro (17-9) earned his UFC contract through the Contender Series and brings a finish-heavy style with no single martial art as his base. He has, however, been stopped in both of his previous UFC outings — by Azamat Murzakanov and then Oumar Sy — making this fight a must-win to stay on the roster. He is a capable and experienced veteran, but the oddsmakers and the evidence see this as a severe mismatch.

Pick: Abdul Rakhman Yakhyaev

Method: KO/TKO

The UFC is building Yakhyaev and has handed him a highlight reel on a silver platter. Ribeiro is a serviceable opponent but has been stopped twice in the UFC already and is the kind of veteran the promotion feeds to prospects they are investing in. This fight is not going the distance. Yakhyaev is going to walk into the cage, do what he does, and the referee is going to stop it in round one.

Rafael Estevam vs. Ethyn Ewing

Rafael Estevam (14-0) is 3-0 in the UFC, with all three wins coming by decision on the back of relentless wrestling and top control. He earned his deal via the Contender Series and has not lost a professional fight across 14 bouts. The issue surrounding this matchup is that Estevam has missed weight again — a recurring problem that has become his defining storyline and one that will likely determine his long-term future in the promotion regardless of what happens Saturday.

Ethyn Ewing (9-2) made a stunning short-notice UFC debut at UFC 322 in November 2025, upsetting Malcolm Wellmaker by decision at Madison Square Garden. A wrestler by background who fights primarily as a boxer, he enters on a nine-fight win streak and looking to prove his debut performance was no fluke.

Pick: Rafael Estevam

Method: Decision

This is a frustrating fight to have to pick. Estevam missing weight again is a legitimate reason to be annoyed at the matchmaking — he stands to win in an illegitimate fashion if the scales are any indication of where his body is heading. That said, the hype around Ewing deserves scrutiny. Wellmaker was massively overhyped heading into that MSG fight, and his biggest claim to fame before Ewing was a first-round KO of Kris Moutinho — not exactly a murderers' row. Fighters who rack up quick first-round finishes sometimes don't know what to do when a fight goes past the opening minutes, and that is exactly what happened at MSG. Ewing seized the moment, but the opponent may have been more fragile than advertised. Estevam, by contrast, has proven he can grind through a full fight and impose his wrestling at the top level. He is the more seasoned UFC fighter, is clearly adding size since his move up from flyweight, and has a proven blueprint for winning. Ewing is game, but this is too much too soon. The irony is that Estevam will likely be cut regardless of the outcome.

Tommy McMillen vs. Manolo Zecchini

Tommy McMillen (9-0) earned his UFC deal on Dana White's Contender Series in September 2025 and makes his promotional debut here. He lands 7.53 significant strikes per minute at 51% accuracy, combines relentless pace with active submission hunting, and has used the guillotine choke to finish multiple opponents. He is installed as roughly a -1000 favorite.

Manolo Zecchini (11-4) last competed in September 2023, returning after more than two and a half years of inactivity. That layoff is the defining fact of his participation in this fight. He has no business being on a main card in 2026, and his presence here is an absolute layup gift for a debutant.

Pick: Tommy McMillen

Method: Decision

What this fight is doing on a main card is a genuine mystery. McMillen barely cracked the UFC off the Contender Series and is probably only here because of his connection to Sean O'Malley's camp through Tim Welch. To Welch's credit, he generally does a reasonable job of not rushing his fighters — and McMillen getting Zecchini, a guy who hasn't thrown a punch in competition in over two and a half years, is about as soft a debut as you can get. Zecchini is an absolute can. McMillen wins this comfortably.

Robert Ruchała vs. José Delano

Robert Ruchała (11-2), the 27-year-old former KSW interim featherweight champion from Poland, made an unsuccessful UFC debut with a unanimous decision loss to William Gomis in September 2025. His KSW pedigree is legitimate, but he has yet to show that his regional-circuit success translates to the world stage. This is his second UFC opportunity and something close to a must-win.

José Delano (16-3) earned his UFC contract on the Contender Series in August 2025 and makes his promotional debut on a four-fight winning streak. The 29-year-old LFA standout brings genuine finishing ability — a rear naked choke over Alan Villalba, a knockout over Abu Muslim Alikhanov — into his first octagon appearance. Fresh momentum versus a fighter trying to prove his UFC debut loss was an aberration.

Pick: José Delano

Method: Decision

Ruchała is here more by circumstance than merit — his roster spot feels like it was extended to give him a fair second chance rather than earned on the basis of his debut performance. Delano, by contrast, has rightfully earned his place in the promotion and arrives with genuine finishing power and four consecutive wins. The momentum is entirely on his side. Ruchała may be the more experienced name, but Delano is the hungrier fighter in this matchup, and that edge is enough to take the decision.

Prelims Picks

Thomas Petersen vs. Guilherme Pat

Thomas Petersen (10-4), a two-time Minnesota State wrestling champion, has gone 2-3 in his last five — most recently suffering a KO loss to Vitor Petrino in October 2025 that snapped a short win streak. That loss has aged reasonably well, as Petrino went on to style on Steven Asplund in his next outing. Petersen is a tested UFC veteran who has faced significantly better competition than anyone on Pat's résumé.

Guilherme Pat (6-0), a 6'5" Brazilian heavyweight with a 6'9" reach, had to overcome multiple US visa denials before finally reaching the UFC, keeping his unbeaten record intact throughout. He most recently earned a unanimous decision over Allen Frye Jr. in December 2025. His only UFC fight was against another debutant who did not even come through the Contender Series — he has not yet been tested by a seasoned UFC fighter.

Pick: Thomas Petersen

Method: KO/TKO

Pat is an interesting physical specimen, but his UFC experience consists of one fight against another raw debutant. He has not felt the pressure of a seasoned octagon veteran who knows how to pace himself, exploit openings, and weather adversity — and Petersen, whatever his inconsistencies, is exactly that kind of fighter. The Petrino loss looks less damning now that Petrino has proven he can compete at a high level. Pat has not felt the wrath of a seasoned UFC fight yet, and he is in for a rude awakening Saturday night. The wrestling pedigree and experience gap are too significant. Petersen gets the finish.

Alessandro Costa vs. Stewart Nicoll

Alessandro Costa (14-5) took a short-notice matchup against Amir Albazi back in 2022 to get into the UFC and has been streaky ever since — winning one, losing one in a pattern that has kept him perpetually on the roster bubble. He enters as the -400 favorite coming off a KO loss to Alden Coria in September 2025. He has faced elite flyweight competition in Erceg and Albazi, and while he has come up short against the top tier, he has shown genuine finishing ability along the way.

Stewart Nicoll (8-2) is 0-2 in the UFC, having dropped decisions to Lucas Rocha and been submitted by Jesus Aguilar in his first two octagon appearances. Both opponents were mid-level at best, which makes his inability to get a win all the more telling. His roster status is in serious jeopardy heading into this fight.

Pick: Alessandro Costa

Method: KO/TKO

Nicoll is simply not good enough to be in the UFC. He went 0-2 against mid-level opponents and has nothing to show for his time in the promotion. Costa, for all his inconsistency, is on a different level than anything Nicoll has faced. He has fought legitimate contenders and has the finishing power to end this in convincing fashion. Costa is going out there and demolishing Nicoll — this is a bad stylistic and quality matchup for the Scotsman.

Lando Vannata vs. Darrius Flowers

Lando Vannata (12-7-2) returns to the octagon after a three-year absence, having last competed in April 2023. Known throughout his UFC career for unorthodox movement, creative spinning attacks, and producing some of the most entertaining fights on the card, he has also been defined by inconsistency. The long layoff is the central question mark heading into his return.

Darrius Flowers (12-8-1) is on a three-fight losing skid and desperately needs a win to avoid being cut. A Chicago-based fighter with explosive power and relentless aggression, he has been stopped or out-grappled in each of his last three losses. His opponents in those defeats — Evan Elder, Michael Johnson, and Jake Matthews — are a more respectable collection than what Vannata has been losing to lately.

Pick: Darrius Flowers

Method: Decision

The pick here is simple: I refuse to pick someone who has lost to Daniel Zellhuber. Vannata's most recent loss, a unanimous decision to Zellhuber in April 2023, is disqualifying on its own. Flowers has been on a rough run, but his opponents have been legitimately more dangerous than anyone who ends up losing to Zellhuber. Three years of inactivity doesn't help Vannata's case either. Flowers gets the win.

Hailey Cowan vs. Alice Pereira

Hailey Cowan (7-4) is 0-2 in the UFC since 2023 and has yet to win inside the octagon. Her most recent outing was a submission loss to Nora Cornolle in April 2025, and at this point her roster spot is hanging by a thread. She is the more experienced fighter on paper, but UFC experience means little when you have nothing to show for it.

Alice Pereira (5-1) made history as the youngest woman ever to fight in the UFC when she debuted against Montserrat Rendon at Noche UFC in September 2025, dropping a competitive split decision in her first octagon appearance. At just 20 years old, she is one of the UFC's most touted young prospects, with four finishes in her pre-UFC record including two stoppages inside the first fifteen seconds.

Pick: Alice Pereira

Method: Decision

Cowan probably shouldn't be in the UFC. She has gone 0-2 against underwhelming competition and has given no indication that a turnaround is coming. Pereira, despite the debut loss to Rendon, had a genuinely competitive fight against a solid opponent and showed she belongs in the promotion. She is significantly younger, carries finishing power, and has the upside that Cowan simply does not. Pereira gets the win here and takes another step toward establishing herself in the division.

Azamat Bekoev vs. Tresean Gore

Azamat Bekoev (20-4), the former LFA middleweight champion now training out of American Top Team, had his 8-fight win streak snapped by Yousri Belgaroui in October 2025 — stopped by punches in the third round. Prior to that loss, he had strung together wins over Ryan Loder and Zachary Reese, showing genuine finishing power with his right hook and ground-and-pound. He enters as a -565 favorite and is buried surprisingly far down this card.

Tresean Gore (5-4), the TUF 29 finalist, has been stopped or dominated in back-to-back bouts against Marco Tulio and grappling specialist Rodolfo Vieira. His UFC future looks precarious, and this fight represents a last chance to avoid the chopping block.

Pick: Azamat Bekoev

Method: KO/TKO

It is puzzling that Bekoev is this far down the card given his pedigree and the quality of his recent wins. Gore is simply garbage at this level — he has been handled by mid-tier opponents and has shown no sign of putting it together consistently. Bekoev is going to go out there and brutalize Gore in the opening round. The only mystery is why this fight isn't higher on the card.

Dione Barbosa vs. Melissa Gatto

Dione Barbosa (8-4) is a seven-time Brazilian national judo champion with black belts in both judo and BJJ, and is 2-2 in the UFC. She is coming off a unanimous decision loss to Karine Silva at UFC 319 in August 2025 — a legitimate opponent, which softens the sting of that result. Her grappling credentials are elite, but she has struggled to impose them consistently against the best in the flyweight division.

Melissa Gatto (9-2-2) last competed in May 2024, meaning she returns after nearly two years away. A Brazilian flyweight known for knockout power and striking dynamism — including body kick finishes — she has been inconsistent across her UFC tenure and has had trouble stringing wins together at the elite level. The near two-year layoff is the defining variable in this matchup.

Pick: Dione Barbosa

Method: Decision

Neither woman has done anything truly spectacular in the UFC, but the edge here goes to Barbosa on the basis of activity and opponent quality. Losing to Karine Silva is nothing to be ashamed of — Silva is a legitimate contender. Gatto, on the other hand, is coming back after nearly two years out of competition, and ring rust at this level is real. Barbosa's elite judo and grappling base give her a path to control the fight and grind out the decision, and Gatto is unlikely to be sharp enough off the layoff to stop her.

Dakota Hope vs. Kai Kamaka III

Dakota Hope (11-1) makes his UFC debut on a five-fight win streak, most recently stopping Mauricio Gomez with punches in the first round in February 2026. The 29-year-old LFA and Fury FC veteran was added to the card on short notice and faces a significant step up in competition for his first octagon appearance.

Kai Kamaka III (17-7-1) re-signed with the UFC on five days' notice after spending time in Bellator and PFL following his initial release, going 6-2 in those promotions. He notched a Fight of the Night win over Tony Kelley in his original UFC debut and most recently knocked out Michel Lima in January 2026. The problem: he was also released after losing a fight to someone in Tuff-N-Uff in October, which is not the kind of result that inspires confidence in his current standing.

Pick: Dakota Hope

Method: Decision

This one is genuinely hard to read given the short-notice nature of both fighters' involvement, but the deciding factor is straightforward — I will not pick someone who was released from the UFC, went to Bellator and PFL, and then lost a fight to someone competing in Tuff-N-Uff before getting a call-up. That October loss is disqualifying. Hope is on a five-fight win streak, is the fresher fighter, and carries the momentum of a man who has earned his spot. Hope gets the decision.
